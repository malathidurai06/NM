APJ.ABDUL KALAM

A.P.J. Abdul Kalam was an Indian aerospace scientist and statesman who served as the 11th President of India from 2002 to 2007. He is widely regarded as the "People's President". Born in Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu, he had a distinguished career as a scientist, primarily at the Defence Research and Development Organisation (DRDO) and the Indian Space Research Organisation (ISRO). He played a pivotal role in India's civilian space program and military missile development, earning him the moniker "Missile Man of India". Kalam also played a crucial role in the Pokhran-II nuclear tests in 1998.

Early Life and Education

Scientific Career

  • Worked on the development of India's first indigenous satellite launch vehicle (SLV-III).
  • Played a key role in the development of the Agni and Prithvi missiles.
  • Served as the Chief Scientific Adviser to the Prime Minister and Secretary of the DRDO.
  • Led the Pokhran-II nuclear tests in 1998.
